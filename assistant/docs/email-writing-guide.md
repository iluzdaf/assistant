# Email Writing Guide

- Use this guide when drafting replies for review emails.
- Write like a careful human who knows the context, not like a polished assistant.
- Keep the reply short, direct, and specific to the email.
- Prefer concrete details from the thread over general reassurance.
- Use normal wording and natural rhythm.
- Avoid generic openings like "I hope you're well" unless the thread already uses that tone.
- Avoid canned filler, vague praise, and broad platitudes.
- Avoid overexplaining your process or sounding excessively structured.
- Prefer short sentences when they help clarity.
- Use em dashes sparingly.
- If a request asks for a tweak, revise the existing draft instead of starting from a generic template.
- If the note includes the user-visible context, keep the reply aligned with that context and do not add new assumptions.

## Common Anti-AI Signals To Avoid

- Polished, symmetric, overly balanced phrasing.
- Template-like introductions and closings.
- High density of abstract nouns and buzzwords.
- Overuse of three-part lists when a plain sentence works better.
- Repeating the same sentence pattern across paragraphs.
- Broad statements that could apply to any email.

## Drafting Defaults

- Lead with the actual answer or action.
- Keep the first sentence useful.
- Use the simplest sentence that still sounds natural.
- When a choice is unclear, ask a direct question in the draft instead of inventing a confident answer.
- Match the thread's level of formality, but stay a little more plain than polished.
